Print Plan
Demo profile: John Doe is a fictional patient created for planning demonstration only.

Client Snapshot

  • Name: John Doe
  • Age: 82
  • Location: Quincy, MA
  • Status: Widowed, lives alone in 2-story home
  • Primary concerns: Mild cognitive decline, fall risk, medication mismanagement, social isolation

Clinical + Functional Summary

  • Hypertension, Type 2 diabetes, osteoarthritis, early-stage dementia
  • 2 falls in last 6 months (no major fracture)
  • ADLs: independent in feeding and toileting; needs cueing for bathing/dressing
  • IADLs: requires support for medication setup, transportation, finances, meal planning

Primary Goals (Family + Client)

Prevent hospitalization/falls Maintain home independence safely Improve medication adherence Reduce caregiver stress on adult children Create clear escalation plan

Short-Term Plan (0–30 Days)

  1. Safety first (Week 1): Home safety audit, remove rugs/clutter, install grab bars, night lights, stair rails check.
  2. Medication control (Week 1): Pharmacy blister packs + locked dispenser + family med log.
  3. Medical alignment (Week 1-2): PCP follow-up, medication reconciliation, orthostatic BP check, PT referral for gait/balance.
  4. Care coverage (Week 2): Start 20-25 hrs/week home care aide (AM routines, meals, supervision, companionship).
  5. Family command center (Week 2): Shared caregiver calendar, weekly sibling update, emergency contacts one-pager on fridge.

Mid-Term Plan (30–90 Days)

  1. Increase structured routine (meals, hydration, exercise, sleep consistency).
  2. Enroll in adult day health program 1-2 days/week for stimulation + respite.
  3. Formal cognitive and mood check; adjust dementia care approach.
  4. Reassess care hours every 30 days based on incidents and caregiver observations.
  5. Review legal docs: HCP proxy, HIPAA release, POA, MOLST preferences discussion.

Long-Term Plan (3–12 Months)

  1. Progressive care pathway: Home care → enhanced home care → memory care placement trigger if safety fails.
  2. Trigger thresholds: 2+ falls/quarter, wandering event, med refusal >3x/week, nighttime confusion escalation.
  3. Financial roadmap: Estimate monthly cost bands and benefits review (VA, LTC policy, MassHealth planning consult).
  4. Quarterly care conference: Family + geriatric care manager + PCP team.

Recommended Care Team

  • Geriatric Care Manager (lead coordinator)
  • Primary Care Physician + Nurse
  • Home Health Aide / Companion
  • Physical Therapist (fall prevention)
  • Elder Law Attorney (documents)
  • Family Point Person (oldest daughter as default)

Family Communication Protocol

  • Daily: aide log + medication confirmation
  • Weekly: 20-min family huddle (wins, issues, adjustments)
  • Monthly: outcome review (falls, ER visits, weight, mood)
  • Urgent escalation: chest pain, acute confusion, head strike, sudden weakness → 911
